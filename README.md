# 💹PROJECT SUMMARY💹 #
This project is based on the concept of being a Student Digital Ambassador, and aims to assist students in using the digital world safely and effectively. This project will cover many necessary digital skills, such as how to create an appropriate professional image online and identifying and preventing cyber threats, both of which are very important for college students today.
This project has been divided into five modules, each addressing different aspects of digital literacy. The module will consist of tasks to complete, supporting files, screenshots, and reflections that will be stored in this GitHub repository as well as a comprehensive project report. This project allowed me to gain hands-on experience with different types of professional platforms and tools, but most importantly, it taught me more about being safe when communicating digitally, being ethical online, and having an understanding of cybersecurity.

1️⃣Module 1: Digital Literacy Infographic1️⃣
In this module I developed an infographic to summarize the main concepts of digital literacy for students and include information about the definition of digital literacy, safe internet browsing practices, how to manage one’s digital identity, and how to develop a professional online personality. I tried to emphasize visual appeal and ease-of-use for the audience, while also developing my design-thinking skills through translating complex ideas into simple and informative visuals.

2️⃣Module 2: Digital Portfolio2️⃣
In this module I created and arranged my professional digital profile on social media sites, such as GitHub and LinkedIn. I created a README file for my GitHub profile that introduces my background, area of study, and educational goals. On LinkedIn, I have filled out the Education section with my degree and a short description of my academic history. This module showed me the power of having an organized digital portfolio in positively impacting how equal, instructors, and future employers will see students. This further reinforced that all college students should maintain their digital portfolios starting when they first begin attending college.

3️⃣Module 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms3️⃣
In this module I explored two main types of digital tools that are essential for successful coding and coding collaboration. The first are coding practice websites and second are cloud-based collaborative tools.

4️⃣Module IV - Digital Communication and Email Etiquette4️⃣
This module focused on the study and practice of professional digital communication through creating two types of formal emails (one professional and one for internship purposes). Additionally, I created a list of Do's and Don'ts for social media and online responsibility which was designed for college students. The learning from this module was I learned that both how you communicate (digital etiquette) and what you say all have a significant impact on the level of professionalism to individuals who receive your communication. 

5️⃣Module V - Cyber Crime Awareness5️⃣
In this module, I researched (using real life examples) a case of everyday cyber crime (specifically UPI fraud/online fraud etc.) to complete a detailed case study on how these crimes occur as well as the groups of people that commit these crimes and who these criminals target. I also developed a list of prevention measures and steps to report cyber crimes to the National Cyber Crime Portal (www.cybercrime.gov.in) and call the National Helpline (1930) with at least two of the prevention measures specific to UPI and fraud prevention . The learning from this module led me to become very aware of digital crime and the actions I can take to help protect myself from becoming a victim of digital crime.
